When something goes wrong in your rental, knowing whether it's a true emergency — and what to do in the first few minutes — can prevent a small problem from becoming a big one. Here's a simple guide for our tenants.

## What counts as an emergency

Call our 24/7 emergency line immediately for anything that threatens safety or is actively causing major damage, such as:

- Flooding or a major water leak you can't stop
- No air conditioning during dangerous heat (or no heat during a cold snap)
- A gas smell or suspected gas leak
- Electrical hazards, sparking, or burning smells
- A fire (call 911 first), or a break-in or other safety threat
- A sewage backup or no running water

For a gas leak or fire, get to safety and call 911 first — then call us.

## What's *not* an emergency

These matter, but they can wait for a normal maintenance request: a dripping faucet, a single non-working outlet, a loose handle, an appliance quirk, or minor cosmetic issues. Submit these through the maintenance request form and we'll schedule them promptly.

## First steps in a real emergency

1. **Make it safe.** For gas or fire, evacuate and call 911. For flooding, shut off the water at the fixture or main valve if you can do so safely.
2. **Stop the damage.** Move belongings away from water; turn off a breaker if there's an electrical issue.
3. **Call us right away** at our 24/7 line — don't wait or use the online form for emergencies.
4. **Document it.** A few quick photos help us send the right help fast.

## The bottom line

When in doubt, call. We'd much rather hear from you early than have a small issue turn into a costly one. Save our number in your phone so it's there when you need it.
