Two of the most popular markets for Tampa Bay rental investors sit right next to each other along I-75: New Tampa in Hillsborough County and Wesley Chapel just north in Pasco. Both are strong — but they suit slightly different strategies.

## New Tampa: established and stable

New Tampa's master-planned communities — Tampa Palms, Hunter's Green, Cory Lake Isles — are mature, gated, and amenity-rich, with A-rated schools and proximity to USF and Advent Health. For investors, that translates to:

- Reliable, long-term tenant demand from professionals and families
- Stable, predictable rents
- Slightly higher entry prices, with less room for rapid appreciation

If you want a dependable, low-drama hold in an area that's already proven, New Tampa is hard to beat.

## Wesley Chapel: growth and newer inventory

Wesley Chapel is one of Florida's fastest-growing areas, with new construction, the Shops at Wiregrass, new hospitals, and attractions like the Epperson Crystal Lagoon. For investors, that means:

- Newer homes with lower maintenance costs
- Strong demand from a wave of relocating families
- More upside tied to continued growth — with the trade-off of more new supply competing for tenants

If you want newer inventory and growth potential, Wesley Chapel is compelling.

## So which one?

Choose **New Tampa** for stability and a proven, established market. Choose **Wesley Chapel** for newer homes, lower upkeep, and growth exposure. Many investors who can swing it like owning in both to balance their portfolio.
